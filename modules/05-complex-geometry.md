# Module 05: Complex Geometry - Scaling With Phase

## Core Question

What changes when scaling can also rotate?

## Limitation

Real scalars stretch and flip. They do not encode phase naturally.

## New Object

Complex scalars and complex vector spaces.

## Legal Operations

- Multiply by a complex number to scale and rotate.
- Use complex conjugation.
- Define Hermitian inner products.
- Study unitary transformations that preserve complex geometry.

## Invariants

- Magnitude |z|
- Phase modulo 2pi
- Hermitian norm
- Orthogonality under conjugate symmetry
- Eigenvalues of Hermitian operators are real

## Concrete Example

Multiplying by i rotates the complex plane by 90 degrees:

```text
i(a + bi) = -b + ai
```

The scalar did not merely stretch the vector. It changed direction through phase.

## Hand Exercises

1. Compute (1 + i)(2 - 3i).
2. Write 1 + i in polar form.
3. Explain why the complex inner product uses conjugation.
4. Show that a Hermitian 1 by 1 matrix must contain a real number.

## Depends On

Number systems, vectors, inner products, and matrices.

## Supports Later

Hermitian operators, Fourier analysis, representation theory, quantum formalism, and spectral theory.
