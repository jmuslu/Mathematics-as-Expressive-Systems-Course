# Module 04: Invariants - What Survives Transformation

## Core Question

When a system changes, what remains recognizable?

## Limitation

If every transformation is just motion, stretching, or mixing, then we need a way to identify stable features.

## New Object

An invariant: a quantity, subspace, relation, or property that remains unchanged under a chosen class of transformations.

## Legal Operations

- Ask whether two objects are equivalent.
- Classify transformations.
- Detect preserved directions.
- Compare objects without depending on coordinates.

## Invariants

This module is about invariants themselves:

- Rank survives row and column basis changes.
- Determinant measures signed volume scaling.
- Trace is stable under similarity.
- Eigenvalues are stable under change of basis.
- Inner products may be preserved by orthogonal or unitary transformations.

## Concrete Example

If A and B are similar, meaning B = P^-1 A P, then A and B describe the same transformation in different coordinate systems. Their matrix entries may differ, but their eigenvalues match.

## Hand Exercises

1. Find the determinant and trace of a 2 by 2 diagonal matrix.
2. Explain what determinant 0 means geometrically.
3. Give one property of a matrix that changes when you change basis and one that does not.

## Depends On

Matrices and basis change.

## Supports Later
