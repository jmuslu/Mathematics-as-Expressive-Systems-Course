# Module 02: Vectors and Spaces - Direction, Combination, Reachability

## Core Question

What can be reached by combining directions?

## Limitation

A scalar can say "how much," but not "in what direction." Many problems require multiple degrees of freedom at once.

## New Object

A vector space: a collection of objects that can be added together and scaled.

## Legal Operations

- Add vectors.
- Scale vectors.
- Form linear combinations.
- Ask whether a target lies in a span.
- Change basis without changing the underlying vector.

## Invariants

- Dimension
- Linear independence
- Span
- Basis size
- Subspace structure

## Concrete Example

If u = (1, 0) and v = (0, 1), then every point (a, b) in R^2 can be written as au + bv.

The basis vectors do not just store numbers. They define what counts as a reachable direction.

## Hand Exercises

1. Decide whether (3, 5) is in the span of (1, 1) and (1, -1).
2. Find two different bases for R^2.
3. Explain why a basis is a coordinate system, not just a list of vectors.
