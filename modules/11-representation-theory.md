# Module 11: Representation Theory - Symmetry As Computation

## Core Question

How can abstract legal moves become actual machines?

## Limitation

Groups describe symmetries abstractly, but computation often requires concrete linear transformations.

## New Object

A representation of a group G on a vector space V: a rule assigning each group element to an invertible linear map on V while preserving composition.

## Legal Operations

- Turn group elements into matrices.
- Decompose a representation into simpler pieces.
- Compute characters.
- Use linear algebra to study symmetry.

## Invariants

- Dimension of a representation
- Character
- Irreducibility
- Decomposition into irreducibles
- Fixed subspaces

## Concrete Example

The cyclic group C4 can act on R^2 by rotations through 0, 90, 180, and 270 degrees. The abstract move becomes a rotation matrix.

## Hand Exercises

1. Write the 2 by 2 matrix for rotation by 90 degrees.
2. Verify that applying it four times gives the identity matrix.
3. Explain why this is a representation of C4.

## Depends On

Groups, matrices, eigenvectors, complex numbers, and invariant subspaces.

## Supports Later

Harmonic analysis, quantum theory, crystallography, Lie theory, and modern algebra.
